WHAT'S MUN?

Model United Nations, also known as Model UN or MUN, is an extra-curricular activity in which students typically roleplay delegates to the United Nations and simulate UN committees. This activity takes place at MUN conferences, which is usually organized by a high school or college MUN club. At the end of most conferences, outstanding delegates in each committee are recognized and given an award certificate; the Best Delegate in each committee, however, receives a gavel.

CMC'S TEAM

Claremont McKenna College Model United Nations (CMCMUN) is an award-winning and nationally ranked organization representing the College on both national and international stages. Nearly 50 students apply annually for the highly competitive interdisciplinary team, comprised of 35 members across class years and major disciplines.

Competing on the CMCMUN team allows students to hone leadership, communication, and critical problem solving skills that help them gain jobs and fellowships after graduation. Team members are given the opportunity to travel to several conferences each semester to substantively debate topics with undergraduates from around the world. Additionally, anyone on campus can be involved in CMCMUN programing through McKennaMUN, our high school conference. For McKennaMUN students can manage logistics and finances for a conference that brings hundreds of students to CMC and give back to the community through mentorship and volunteering with MUN programs at local high schools.
